Biography

I am a Lecturer in Embedded and Intelligent Systems at the University of Essex, and I am part of the Robotics and Embedded Systems Research Group. My interests include IoT, Edge Computing, ML, Signal Processing, Federated Learning and applications to healthcare. I also explore high-order tensor decompositions, multimodal learning, explainable AI, and energy-efficient AI. I lead the Internet of Things and Machine Intelligence Lab within the Future Health Technologies cluster and serve as an EPSRC reviewer, EU expert, and editor at PLOS ONE.

Previously, I was a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at Essex, a Research Associate at King’s College London, and a Research Fellow at the University of Surrey. I received my BSc from Near East University, MSc from Essex, and PhD from QMUL (EPSRC-funded), focused on semantic audio-based ontology generation.
