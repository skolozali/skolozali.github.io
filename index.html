Research

My research interests include Internet of Things, Edge Computing, Machine Learning, Time Series Analysis, Signal Processing, Semantic Web, and Federated Learning. I am also focused on high-order tensor decompositions, multi-aspect learning, multimodal learning, explainable AI, and energy-efficient AI technologies for healthcare applications.

Supervision

I have supervised several MSc students and previously supervised PhD student Thanh Dat Ngo. I am always happy to hear from motivated PhD candidates—please do not hesitate to contact me if you're interested in working on intelligent systems, multimodal learning, or healthcare monitoring technologies.

Background

I am a Lecturer in Embedded and Intelligent Systems at the University of Essex and part of the Robotics and Embedded Systems Research Group. I lead the Internet of Things and Machine Intelligence Lab, within the Future Health Technologies cluster. My previous roles include:

  • Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the Institute of Analytics and Data Science, University of Essex
  • Research Associate in Applied Big Data Analysis at the MRC-PHE Centre for Environment and Health, King's College London
  • Research Fellow at the 5G Innovation Centre, University of Surrey (EU CityPulse project)

I serve as an editor at PLOS ONE and act as a reviewer for the EPSRC, the European Commission, and numerous journals including the IEEE Internet of Things Journal, ACM Transactions on Internet of Things, IEEE Transactions on Emerging Topics in Computing, IEEE Transactions on Industrial Informatics, and Neural Computing and Applications. I am also a member of the EPSRC Peer Review College and a registered expert with the European Commission.
